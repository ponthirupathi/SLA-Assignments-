Learning is the lifelong process of acquiring new knowledge, skills, understanding, and values through experience, study, or practice. It is an active process that involves encountering new information, paying attention to it, and integrating it with existing knowledge to form long-lasting changes in behavior and performance. The process of learning Acquisition (Encoding): The initial stage where new information is first obtained and processed. Retention (Storage): The process of transferring information into long-term memory so it is not forgotten. Recall (Retrieval): The ability to access and use stored information when it is needed. Key aspects of learning Ongoing process: Learning is

not confined to formal education; it happens throughout life. Active and multi-step: Learning requires active participation, including encountering, processing, and applying new information. Results in lasting change: The changes from learning often last a lifetime and impact personality, attitudes, and skills.

Driven by experience: Learning is a result of experience and interaction with the world. Can be influenced by social, cultural, and emotional factors: These variables can affect the learning process. Mistakes are valuable: During practice, mistakes are positive indicators that allow for correction and improvement before actual performance. How learning occurs Formal education: Through structured environments like schools and universities. Informal methods: By reading books, exploring new places, and engaging in hobbies. Technology: Using online resources, search engines, and other digital tools. Observation and practice: By observing others and practicing new behaviors and skills
